Trans Name Changes & Baby Name Trends

Do trans people function as cultural early adopters in naming? A study of 149,602 Ontario Gazette records (2000–2024).

Mean cross-correlation lag by group

Positive = name changes precede baby naming trends; negative = name changes follow trends

Primary Finding

Across all gazette name changes, the mean lag is −0.30 years — people generally follow current naming trends. But in the trans-coded subset, this flips to +0.86 years: trans name changes appear in the gazette before those names peak in baby data.

Important Caveat

The female trans positive lag (+1.71y) is likely a statistical artifact: trans women tend to choose 1970s–80s female names (JESSICA, JENNIFER) that were already declining. Two declining series can produce a spurious lag. Granger causality tests are non-significant. See the About tab.
